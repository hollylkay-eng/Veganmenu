Nutrition Plan

7-Day Vegan
Meal Plan

A wholesome week of plant-based eating, carefully crafted to keep saturated fat very low — no coconut products, maximum nourishment.

🌱 100% Vegan 💚 Very Low Sat. Fat 🚫 No Coconut 👤 Serves 1
01 Fresh Beginnings
🌅Breakfast
Overnight Oats with Berries & Flaxseed
Rolled oats soaked in oat milk with chia seeds, topped with mixed berries, sliced banana, and ground flaxseed. A drizzle of maple syrup for sweetness.
~1g sat fat High fibre Omega-3
☀️Lunch
Lentil & Vegetable Soup with Rye Bread
Hearty red lentil soup with diced tomatoes, spinach, carrot, celery, and cumin. Served with a slice of rye bread. No added oils needed — naturally rich and filling.
~0.3g sat fat High protein Iron-rich
🍎Snack
Apple with Almond Butter
Sliced apple with 1 tbsp natural almond butter (low in saturated fat compared to other nut butters).
~0.5g sat fat Vitamin E
🌙Dinner
Chickpea & Spinach Stir-Fry with Brown Rice
Chickpeas sautéed with garlic, fresh ginger, tomatoes, turmeric, and garam masala. Wilted spinach stirred through. Served over brown rice with a squeeze of lemon.
~0.8g sat fat High protein Antioxidants
🌿 Day 1 estimated total saturated fat: ~2.6g — well below the 10g daily limit for a very low sat-fat diet. All meals are whole-food focused with no added saturated fats.
02 Mediterranean Vibes
🌅Breakfast
Smoothie Bowl with Granola
Blended frozen mango, banana, and oat milk poured into a bowl. Topped with low-fat granola (oat-based, no nuts), kiwi slices, and a sprinkle of pumpkin seeds.
~0.8g sat fat Vitamin C Energising
☀️Lunch
Hummus & Roasted Veggie Wrap
Wholegrain wrap filled with generous hummus, roasted red peppers, courgette, red onion, and rocket. A splash of balsamic glaze for depth.
~1g sat fat High fibre Plant protein
🥒Snack
Veggies & Hummus
Cucumber sticks, carrot batons, and celery with a small pot of hummus for dipping.
~0.3g sat fat Low cal
🌙Dinner
Tomato-Basil Pasta with White Beans
Wholewheat pasta tossed in a rich homemade tomato sauce with cannellini beans, garlic, fresh basil, and a tiny drizzle of olive oil. Topped with nutritional yeast.
~0.6g sat fat Complex carbs Lycopene
🌿 Day 2 estimated total saturated fat: ~2.7g. Olive oil is used sparingly — it's monounsaturated and heart-healthy in small amounts.
03 Asian Inspired
🌅Breakfast
Tofu Scramble on Wholegrain Toast
Crumbled firm tofu cooked with turmeric, garlic, black salt, and nutritional yeast. Served on 2 slices of wholegrain toast with sliced avocado (¼ avocado).
~1.2g sat fat High protein Calcium
☀️Lunch
Miso Soup with Edamame & Soba Noodles
Light white miso broth with soba noodles, edamame, shredded nori, spring onion, and a soft-cooked tofu cube. Seasoned with a dash of low-sodium soy sauce.
~0.5g sat fat Probiotics Complete protein
🍊Snack
Fruit & Seed Mix
Orange segments with a tablespoon of sunflower seeds and a few dried apricots.
~0.2g sat fat Vitamin C & Iron
🌙Dinner
Vegetable Fried Rice with Edamame
Day-old brown rice stir-fried with peas, corn, edamame, carrot, and spring onion in a little sesame oil and soy sauce. Topped with sliced avocado.
~0.9g sat fat Balanced macros Folate
🌿 Day 3 estimated total saturated fat: ~2.8g. Tofu and edamame are excellent low-sat-fat protein sources — and soy has been shown to support heart health.
04 Middle Eastern Warmth
🌅Breakfast
Banana Oat Pancakes with Blueberries
Mashed ripe banana blended with oats and oat milk, cooked in a non-stick pan (no oil). Served with fresh blueberries and a drizzle of agave nectar.
~0.5g sat fat Potassium No added fat
☀️Lunch
Falafel Bowl with Tabbouleh & Tahini Dressing
Baked (not fried) falafel over bulgur wheat with tabbouleh (parsley, tomato, lemon, mint). Light tahini dressing made with lemon juice and water. Cucumber and radish on the side.
~1g sat fat Baked not fried Calcium
🍇Snack
Grapes & Walnuts
A handful of red grapes with 4–5 walnut halves. Walnuts are the highest plant source of omega-3s.
~0.5g sat fat Omega-3 ALA
🌙Dinner
Moroccan-Spiced Vegetable Stew with Couscous
Aubergine, chickpeas, and tomatoes simmered with cinnamon, cumin, coriander, and harissa paste. Served over fluffy wholemeal couscous with fresh coriander and lemon.
~0.6g sat fat Anti-inflammatory High fibre
🌿 Day 4 estimated total saturated fat: ~2.6g. Tahini (sesame paste) has a small amount of saturated fat — the quantity used in this dressing is modest and within limits.
05 Comfort & Colour
🌅Breakfast
Chia Pudding with Mango & Lime
Chia seeds soaked overnight in oat milk with vanilla extract. Topped with fresh diced mango, a squeeze of lime, and a sprinkle of toasted oats.
~0.7g sat fat Omega-3 High fibre
☀️Lunch
Rainbow Grain Bowl with Roasted Veg
Quinoa base with roasted sweet potato, beetroot, red cabbage slaw, cucumber, and cherry tomatoes. Dressed with a lemon-tahini vinaigrette and topped with pumpkin seeds.
~1g sat fat Complete protein Beta-carotene
🥜Snack
Rice Cakes with Peanut Butter
2 plain rice cakes with 1 tbsp natural peanut butter (just peanuts, no palm oil) and a few banana slices.
~0.7g sat fat Protein boost
🌙Dinner
Black Bean Tacos with Corn Salsa
Corn tortillas filled with seasoned black beans (cumin, smoked paprika), fresh corn salsa, shredded lettuce, tomato, and a squeeze of lime. A little guacamole for healthy fats.
~0.8g sat fat Folate & iron Crowd pleaser
🌿 Day 5 estimated total saturated fat: ~3.2g. Peanut butter and avocado do contain small amounts of saturated fat, but are rich in beneficial unsaturated fats — excellent choices overall.
06 Easy Weekend
🌅Breakfast
Avocado Toast with Cherry Tomatoes
Two slices of sourdough bread topped with mashed avocado, halved cherry tomatoes, red pepper flakes, and a squeeze of lemon. Finished with a pinch of flaky salt.
~1g sat fat Heart-healthy fats Vitamin K
☀️Lunch
Lentil & Kale Salad with Mustard Dressing
Puy lentils over massaged kale with roasted cherry tomatoes, shredded carrot, and sunflower seeds. Dressed with Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, and a tiny drizzle of olive oil.
~0.4g sat fat Iron & folate Vitamin K
🫐Snack
Frozen Banana Nice Cream
Blended frozen banana with a splash of oat milk until silky smooth. A natural and indulgent treat with almost no saturated fat.
~0.1g sat fat Sweet treat
🌙Dinner
Stuffed Bell Peppers with Quinoa & Beans
Bell peppers filled with a mixture of quinoa, black beans, sweetcorn, tomato, and herbs. Baked until tender. Topped with a drizzle of hot sauce or fresh salsa.
~0.5g sat fat Oil-free baked Vitamin C & B6
🌿 Day 6 estimated total saturated fat: ~2g. One of the lightest days — perfect for a relaxed weekend where you might be snacking more freely elsewhere.
07 Sunday Celebration
🌅Breakfast
Baked Oat & Berry Porridge
Thick-cut oats baked with oat milk, cinnamon, maple syrup, and mixed berries until golden. Topped with a handful of fresh raspberries and a few flaked almonds.
~0.6g sat fat Beta-glucan Antioxidants
☀️Lunch
Warming Split Pea & Vegetable Soup
Yellow split peas cooked with leeks, carrots, celery, and vegetable stock until thick and creamy — no cream needed. Seasoned with smoked paprika and fresh parsley.
~0.3g sat fat High protein Naturally creamy
🍵Snack
Oat Milk Latte & Medjool Dates
A warm oat milk latte with 2 Medjool dates — a naturally sweet, energy-sustaining afternoon treat.
~0.2g sat fat Natural energy
🌙Dinner
Mushroom & Lentil Bolognese with Spaghetti
A hearty, meaty-textured sauce made with finely chopped mushrooms, green lentils, tomatoes, red wine, and Italian herbs. Served over wholewheat spaghetti with nutritional yeast.
~0.5g sat fat Umami-rich B vitamins
🌿 Day 7 estimated total saturated fat: ~1.6g. A beautiful end to the week — cosy, flavourful, and nourishing. The mushroom bolognese is a crowd-favourite that even non-vegans love.
Tips for Success
🛒
Batch Cook
Cook grains (rice, quinoa, lentils) in bulk on Sunday to save time across the week.
🥛
Choose Oat Milk
Of all plant milks, oat milk is lowest in saturated fat and works great in cooking and coffee.
🫒
Oil Sparingly
Use olive oil in teaspoon amounts. Try water-sautéing or non-stick pans to reduce oil further.
🌱
Boost Protein
Add edamame, tofu, or legumes to any meal to keep protein high without raising saturated fat.
📖
Read Labels
Check nut butters and granola for added palm oil or coconut — common hidden sources.
💧
Stay Hydrated
Aim for 6–8 glasses of water daily. Herbal teas count too and support digestion.
